AT-WILL but UNBREAKABLE

About the Book

At-Will but Unbreakable is a practical guide for anyone navigating the turbulence of modern careers. Drawing on real-world examples, workplace wisdom, and resilience, this book offers clear, practical strategies for rebuilding confidence, redefining purpose, and rising stronger after unexpected change. Combining timeless wisdom from the Bhagavad Gita with insights drawn from modern workplaces and leadership under pressure, it shows readers how to stay grounded and continue to advance their careers despite uncertainty. Whether you've been fired, laid off, demoted, in a career pivot, or in crisis of identity, this book reminds you of one truth: you are tougher than the moment you're in.

It also draws on practical frameworks the author learned at Harvard, including the Congruence Model and Explore–Exploit thinking, to help readers realign their careers while balancing experimentation with sustained progress.

About the Author

Milind Biraidar is an accomplished aerospace leader, systems integrator, and program strategist with more than two decades of experience across some of the world's most demanding industries. He has led multi-million-dollar aircraft propulsion and automotive programs, collaborated with NASA and the U.S. Air Force, and contributed to future-focused solutions in both military and commercial aviation. His career includes leadership roles at Raytheon, Rolls-Royce, Siemens, Ford, GM, Nissan and Benteler Automotive.

Milind's most transformative insights, however, were shaped far from conference rooms. They were defined during periods of personal upheaval, career disruption, and the challenging work of rebuilding identity and direction. His perspective blends technical expertise with deep empathy for the emotional realities of modern work.

A Harvard Business Analytics Program graduate, Mechanical Engineer (BS & MS) by trade, MBA in Finance & International business and certified PMP with Six Sigma Green belt, Milind combines strategic thinking with a deeply human approach to leadership and growth. Through At Will but Unbreakable, he distills hard-earned lessons into practical wisdom, guiding readers through uncertainty, reinvention, and the pursuit of purpose in both life and work.

Apart from writing or leading aerospace programs, Milind is a mentor, public speaker, and advocate for career resilience. He lives in Connecticut with his family, where he finds joy in hiking, playing tennis, coaching youth soccer, and serving in community initiatives.
